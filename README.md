## üîö Conclusion
This experiment shows that medical insurance prices may be predicted with high accuracy using demographic and lifestyle data.

The Random Forest model excels at capturing complicated, non-linear interactions between variables such as age, BMI, and smoking status.  
The model's **R¬≤ score of 0.85** explains most of the variability in insurance expenses, making it appropriate for real-world risk assessment and premium pricing applications.

Key findings from the analysis: **Smoking is the biggest expense driver**.- Age and BMI have a significant impact on insurance premiums.
- Regional concerns have an impact on expenses but serve only as a secondary purpose.

Overall, this solution shows how machine learning can help with **data-driven pricing strategies, cost optimization, and risk segmentation** in the insurance industry.
